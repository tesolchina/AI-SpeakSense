# Yoodli AI — User Experience Overview

This document summarizes the user experience at https://app.yoodli.ai/home as observed from the product flow and primary features.

## Quick Flow

- Sign in / Sign up: Users authenticate to get to a personalized dashboard.
- Home / Dashboard: Presents recent sessions, progress highlights, and quick actions to start a practice.
- Start Practice: Choose a speaking exercise (prompt, time limit, or presentation) and record video or audio.
- Recording Session: Live recording UI with countdown, pause/stop controls, and optional prompt visibility.
- Review & Analysis: After recording, the app processes the session and shows playback with analytics.

## Key Screens & Components

- Dashboard: Session summary cards, progress metrics, and shortcuts to common exercises.
- Session Recorder: Camera/mic preview, timer, start/pause/stop, and an on-screen prompt/notes area.
- Session Player: Video playback synced with transcript, scrubbing, and segment jump links.
- Analytics Panel: Visual metrics for filler words, pace, volume, energy, eye contact, and facial expressions.
- AI Feedback / Suggestions: Automated, actionable tips and examples for improvement (e.g., reduce "um", vary pace).
- Goals & Practice Plans: Ability to set targets and follow guided practice paths.
- History & Export: Session history, ability to share or download recordings and transcripts.

## Features & Feedback Types

- Automated scoring: Quantitative metrics (scores or percentiles) for multiple dimensions of speaking.
- Filler-word detection: Highlights occurrences and frequency of filler words/phrases.
- Pace & Pauses: Visual timeline of speaking speed and long pauses.
- Transcript & Timestamps: Speech-to-text transcript with timestamps and clickable segments.
- Visual cues: Face/eye-contact detection and suggestions related to camera presence.
- Examples & Coaching Tips: Example rephrases and short exercises to address weaknesses.
- Live Practice/Peer Review (if available): Options to practice with others or get human feedback.

## Interaction Patterns

- Immediate feedback loop: Record → Analyze → Review → Practice again.
- Actionable highlights: Click a metric or transcript segment to see recommendations and jump to that part of the video.
- Guided drills: Short focused exercises targeting specific metrics (e.g., ‘‘fillers’’ drill).

## Accessibility & Settings

- Microphone and camera permissions requested at first use.
- Settings for video/audio quality and privacy (control over saving/sharing sessions).
- Text transcripts available for review; likely keyboard navigable controls for playback.

## Privacy & Data Handling (UX implications)

- Clear UI controls to keep sessions private, share explicitly, or delete recordings.
- Export/download options for user ownership of content.

## Tone & Design

- Friendly, coaching-first tone with positive reinforcement.
- Visual emphasis on progress and clear, digestible analytics rather than raw data overload.

## Typical User Journey (example)

1. Sign in → Land on dashboard with progress snapshot.
2. Choose "Practice" or a guided exercise.
3. Record a 2–5 minute response using the in-app recorder.
4. Review playback with transcript; click on flagged filler words.
5. Read AI suggestions, apply a short drill, and re-record to track improvement.
